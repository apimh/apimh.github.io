OUR STORY

While sharing our mental health experiences with each other, we noticed that time and time again we would continue to share how invalidated and misunderstood we felt in spaces that were supposed to be safe and therapeutic for us. We encountered many barriers that were influenced by our cultural identities, and while mental health is being discussed more than ever, the silence still remains to be prevalent within the American Asian, Asian, and Pacific Islander community. 

We not only wanted to share our stories, but create a space where people who identify with us could put the pieces of culture and mental health together through hearing and learning from each other. The way APIs communicate thoughts and emotions are unique to our community and sub-communities. Many of our cultures do not even have the words for the way we feel, so let's create them - here and now.

Let us create a space that our ancestors never had the luxury to be in.

MEET JAZMINE MEET VICTORIA

kapit-bisig

We are so excited to share our weekly newsletter with all of you: Kapit-Bisig.

We decided to name this to remind ourselves and our audience that this is a community for all of us, not just one of us. Kapit-bisig literally means "linking arms" in Tagalog. It is an idiom commonly used to express the need or want to reach a common goal for the community.
